a. What is Morse Code?
Morse code is a method of transferring information in the form of symbols encoded into sequnces of pulses using two pulse lengths, a dit and a dah. The order and number of dits and dahs determines the symbol, with the dead time inbween determining symbol breaks and word breaks.
International Morse Code has 26 Latin letter symbols, 10 numerals, and several other communication symbols. 
b. Who Developed Morse Code? Samuel Morse and Alfred Vail are typically credited with developing the forerunner to modern Morse code.
